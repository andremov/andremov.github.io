Monopoly

Virtual version of the famous board game. Third semester university project for Data Structures I course. Roll dice, purchase properties, buy and sell houses and hotels, mortgage, draw community chest and chance cards.

Gallery

The purpose of this assignment was to learn lists (stack, queue, singly linked list, doubly linked list, multiply linked list, circular linked list).

Also, it worked as a 'work-around' assignment, as we were not allowed the use of arrays or lists made by Java/Oracle.

Based on Monopoly, the application allows for a close to, if not, complete experience of playing Monopoly, without the imminent table flip.

Allows up to 6 players. Computer players were not programmed.

Allows properties to be bought and sold, and houses to be bought and sold, as well as mortgage to be collected from properties.

Reflects these houses bought, as well as the owner of the property, on the map.

Allows, and checks for, monopolies, and reflects them on the respective properties' cards.

Affects the rent of said properties according to houses bought, mortgage status, and monopoly status.

Fully functional community chest and chance cards.

Worked from October 2016 to November 2016 (Approximately 3 weeks).
